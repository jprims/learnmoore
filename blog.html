How many times will your main effect go in the opposite direction?

by

Posted on September 7, 2014 at 9:00 AM


The primary goal for this study was to determine how variations of the true d value would affect the magnitude of main effect going in the opposite direction. To vary the true d value, we manipulated the sample means and standard deviations to move the centers of distributions closer to each other as well as flattening of the distributions to increase the overlaps between the distributions respectively. We expected the magnitude of the main effect going in the opposite direction to be inversely proportional to the true d value. This is based on the reasoning that the random selection of unique subjects from distributions with a smaller true d value would have a higher probability of having samples from extreme ends that show the main effect in the opposite direction.
