Current Projects
Is Overconfidence a Motivated Bias? Experimental Evidence

 Does the motivation to view oneself positively lead people to hold overly positive beliefs about themselves? Our results suggest that the relationship between motivation and excessively positive self-perceptions is neither strong nor simple, but is complex and emerges only under specific conditions.

Data and materials: Open Science Framework


Overconfidence Over the Lifespan

 This project examines correlations between age and different varieties of overconfidence. The results suggest that as people get older, they are more prone to overprecision. Overprecision is the excessive faith that you know the truth. The results do not suggest that age is correlated with overestimation or overplacement.

Data and materials: Open Science Framework


Overconfidence in Geopolitical Forecasts

 The Good Judgment Project examined geopolitical forecasts from hundreds of forecasters over a period of years. How does the relationship between confidence and accuracy develop over this long period of time?


Overconfidence and Surprise  If people go through their lives overconfident about what will happen, they should wind up surprised all the time. Do they? If not, why not?

Judgement in Managerial Decision Making, 8th Edition

Bazerman, M. H. & Moore, D. A. (2012). Judgment in managerial decision making (8th Edition). New York: Wiley.

Behavioral decision research provides many important insights into managerial behavior. From negotiation to investment decisions, the authors weave behavioral decision research into the organizational realm by examining judgment in a variety of managerial contexts.Embedded with the latest research and theories, Managerial Decision Making 8th Edition gives students the opportunity to understand their own decision-making tendencies, learn strategies for overcoming cognitive biases, and become better decision makers.
