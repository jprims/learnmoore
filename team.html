Self-Aggrandizing Autobiographical Sketch

Jennifer Logg

Ph.D. Candidate

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the University of California, Berkeley in the Management of Organizations group at the Haas School of Business. I am interested in when people make biased judgments and how we can help them improve their accuracy. My dissertation tests why and when people rely on advice from algorithms more than other people. My other work focuses on overconfidence and unrealistic optimism in terms of how people perceive their own and others’ performance. Prior to graduate school, I conducted research at Columbia University on decision making and cooperative behavior.


Julia Prims

Lab Manager

I am Julia (JP) Prims, and I am the Lab Manager for the Accuracy Lab. My job involves making sure that the lab stays well-organized. I also assist with data analysis, research design, and a variety of administrative tasks for the Good Judgment Project. I received my Bachelor’s degree in Psychology with a minor in Technology, Arts, and Media from CU Boulder. My research interests include forecasting, overconfidence, how people interact with digital media, and LGBTQIA populations.
